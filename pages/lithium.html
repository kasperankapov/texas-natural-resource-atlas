Lithium in Texas

Explore where lithium resources and prospects are found in Texas, why lithium matters for batteries and critical minerals, how recent exploration has developed, and which mapped lithium records are included in the Texas Natural Resource Atlas.

Overview

Texas lithium is mostly an emerging resource story. The atlas highlights brine, produced-water, and mineralized-rock opportunities rather than a mature statewide lithium mining industry.

Lithium matters because it is used in rechargeable batteries, grid storage, electric vehicles, and other critical-mineral supply chains. In Texas, public attention is focused on East Texas Smackover brines, the Round Top critical-minerals district, and possible lithium recovery from oilfield produced water.

Where Lithium Is Found in Texas

Texas lithium prospects are concentrated in brine and critical-mineral settings rather than traditional hard-rock lithium mines.

Brief Historical Overview

Unlike oil, coal, or uranium, lithium in Texas is not defined by a long legacy of large-scale extraction. The modern Texas lithium story is tied to critical-mineral exploration, brine chemistry, and direct lithium extraction concepts.

Recent efforts have focused on East Texas Smackover brines and co-product opportunities within broader rare earth and critical-mineral projects such as Round Top Mountain. The timeline is therefore best read as a project-development history, not a production history.

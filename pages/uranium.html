Uranium in Texas

Explore where uranium resources and projects are found in Texas, why uranium matters for nuclear fuel, how South Texas uranium development evolved, and which mapped uranium records are included in the Texas Natural Resource Atlas.

Overview

Texas uranium is concentrated mainly in South Texas. The atlas highlights the South Texas Uranium Province, in-situ recovery projects, central processing plants, and permitted or developing project areas.

Uranium matters because it is the raw material for nuclear fuel. Texas uranium is especially tied to sandstone-hosted deposits and in-situ recovery rather than large open-pit operations today.

Where Uranium Is Found in Texas

The major Texas uranium story is in the Gulf Coastal Plain of South Texas, with project areas in and around Karnes, Bee, Goliad, Duval, Live Oak, Brooks, Jim Hogg, and nearby counties.

Brief Historical Overview

Texas uranium exploration and mining developed in the South Texas coastal plain after mid-20th century discoveries. Early activity included conventional mining in some areas, while modern operations focus heavily on in-situ recovery projects and central processing plants.

The recent uranium story includes project restarts, licensed processing facilities, and hub-and-spoke ISR platforms that connect satellite wellfields to central processing facilities.

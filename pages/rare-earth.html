Overview

Texas rare earth activity combines geology and manufacturing. The atlas includes the Round Top Mountain rare earth and critical-minerals deposit, nearby exploration, and several downstream rare earth magnet or separation-related facilities.

Rare earth elements matter because they are used in high-strength permanent magnets, electronics, defense systems, wind turbines, electric vehicles, and other advanced technologies.

    Far West Texas rare earth and critical-minerals deposit area near Sierra Blanca.

  • Trans-Pecos REE-Enriched Igneous Terrane

    Regional geologic setting for REE-enriched intrusive rocks in far West Texas.

  • Fort Worth / Northlake Rare Earth Magnet Corridor

    Downstream rare earth magnet manufacturing and expansion area in North Texas.

Brief Historical Overview

Texas rare earth development is more recent than oil, gas, lignite, or uranium. The Round Top Mountain district became increasingly important as rare earth elements and other critical minerals gained strategic attention.

In the 2020s, Texas rare earth activity expanded beyond the deposit itself into magnet manufacturing, metal/alloy production, recycling, and proposed separation facilities. This makes the Texas rare earth page partly a geology story and partly a supply-chain story.

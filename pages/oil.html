Overview

Texas is the leading oil-producing state in the United States. In 2024, Texas accounted for 43% of U.S. crude oil production, and EIA reports that Texas crude oil production surpassed 2 billion barrels for the second year in a row.

Oil is one of Texas' most important natural resources. It supports the state energy economy, contributes to national energy supply, and is closely connected to major producing basins such as the Permian Basin and Eagle Ford Shale.

Brief Historical Overview

The Texas oil story begins before the modern shale era. Early discoveries existed before 1900, but the 1901 Spindletop discovery near Beaumont transformed oil from a regional curiosity into a major economic force.

In the decades that followed, oil booms spread across North Texas, East Texas, the Panhandle, the Gulf Coast, and West Texas. Later, large fields in the Permian Basin and modern shale development in the Eagle Ford and Permian reshaped Texas oil production again.

Coal/Lignite in Texas

Explore where coal and lignite resources are found in Texas, why lignite mattered to Texas electricity generation, how mining expanded and declined, and which mapped coal/lignite records are included in the Texas Natural Resource Atlas.

Overview

Texas coal resources are mainly lignite. Lignite is a soft, lower-rank coal found close to the surface in parts of the Gulf Coastal Plain.

The Texas lignite story is closely tied to surface mining, mine-mouth power generation, reclamation, and the Wilcox and Jackson groups that host many lignite deposits.

Brief Historical Overview

Commercial coal and lignite mining in Texas began in the late 1880s after earlier small-scale extraction. Public historical summaries list Texas coal production beginning in 1884, with rapid growth through 1901 and a peak around 1913 before petroleum and electricity reduced demand.

Lignite mining later revived with 20th century mine-mouth power generation and surface mining. Many operations have since moved into closure and reclamation as coal-fired generation declined and several legacy mines and plants retired.
